Tiny Astronaut

Two things about me:

  1. I am a really short person who likes tiny things, like eating from tiny bowls with tiny utensils.
  2. I really like space.

Naturally, I was drawn to The Tiny Astronaut. Tiny Astronaut is one of the stickers (sort of like emoji, but bigger and more detailed) available in Google Hangouts. I've been wanting to dress up as this incredibly adorable astronaut for a couple years. It certainly is a deviation from my traditional Halloween theme of "otherworldly and vaguely threatening," but being tiny astronaut is too cute to pass up.

Construction

The helmet

The main component of helmet is a large 12" acrylic sphere.

The window was made with a a 5" acrylic circle, and the board for the electronics was the same.

LED display

This was the most complex part of the project. Controlling a huge array of SMD LEDs is easy peasy. Being able to see beyond them? Something else entirely.

I briefly flirted with something like the CHEMION glasses, but I had niether access to custom PCB equipment nor the will to put huge amounts of effort into making boards the long way. I considered transparent OLED displays, but they're not available for mass market in the west yet, and the only ones I could find on Alibaba were too small. (Alibaba has some of the coolest, most affordable, cutting edge stuff, FYI.)

I eventually came back to custom PCBs, but armed with knowledge of instant inkjet circuit technology. And you might think, "Hey, wasn't that a thing in, like, 2014? What happened to that?" Or, if you weren't in the maker scene around then, "What's on Earth is that?" It's basically loading up an injket printer with extremely expensive, electrically conductive silver ink, and printing circuits with them. It's a shame interest in it died, because no significant developments have been made in the nanoparticle tech used, and it's just as expensive now as it was then.

An inkjet printer I can freely take apart but also know it's in reasonable condition. I didn't have any old inkjet printers lying around, so I had to buy a new one. As far as highly specialized equipment only usable for certain projects, this is pretty affordable. I used this tutorial for modifying a printer.
