Anticipating the reuse of the frame of the Enoch costume, this costume was concieved even before the Enoch costume was completed. The Hair

I want to emphasize that cutting 50 yards of muslin by hand, even with the proper tools, is possibly one of the most tedious things I have ever done, second only to the 3,000 piece chainmail tunic I made out of soda tabs. Fortunately, it was a mindless task, and I had dozens of hours of TV to catch up on.

After cutting about 12 yards, I got very busy and did not resume until a month later. During the month of being stored in a trash bag under my bed, the strips became very curly, which turned out to be exactly what I needed. It was a nice texture (much better than the limp noodles I had before), and they took up more volume, meaning I would need fewer strands to cover the entire costume. In order to replicate the effect on a shorter time span, I spritzed the cut strips with water, put them in a trash bag, and left them outside the sun to steam. The steaming process took about 48 hours, and they keep their curls even after a long time.

Total project time: 270 hours over the course of three months
Cost: $80
