Hi, I'm Watney!

I'm an electronics hobbyist, chemist, costume maker, scifi/fantasy writer, and digital artist.

Wow! Where did you learn all of that?

Save for chemistry, almost everything is self taught. I also take classes for things that interest me as well, which only sometimes coincide with existing hobbies and job things. For example, astronomy, anatomy, and space power systems. I really like learning.

My formal education is a BS in biochemistry from CSULA in 2014. I also completed a thesis, "The Role of Wdr68 in Bmp Signaling" under the supervision of Dr. Robert Nissen. (Sounds like a lot of fun, right?)

Aren't you a tech person? Why a biochemistry degree?

Many years ago, I thought I wanted to go to medical school, so I took the MCAT and GRE, submitted applications, and got as far as attending interviews before deciding I didn't really want to do that.

So I went back to school to learn about computers! I'm currently a full time student working towards a degree in Computer Science.

Don't get me wrong though; I have an immense love for chemistry! Astrobiology is my favorite. Many of the videos on my YouTube channel, NKLOK Labs, focus heavily on chemistry. I also believe it is a fantastic starting point for understanding anything about aything. Except for computers. Unless you're really into the nitty gritty of hardware.

What kind of work do you do?

I mainly tutor in a number of STEM subjects. I also do some consulting and contracting work, mostly programming.

Pretty much everything I do is for fun, even school; I wouldn't doing it if it wasn't!
