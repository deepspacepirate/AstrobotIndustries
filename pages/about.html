Hi, I'm Watney!

I'm a STEM tutor, chemist, electronics hobbyist, costume maker, scifi/fantasy writer, and digital artist.

That's pretty cool. Where did you learn how to do all of that?

It's a mixture of directed coursework and self teaching. This usually consists of taking classes or watching online lectures for things that interest me. These subjects only sometimes coincide with existing hobbies and job things. My current interests are astronomy, anatomy, and quantum mechanics.

My formal education consists of a BS in biochemistry from CSULA in 2014. I also completed a thesis, "The Role of Wdr68 in Bmp Signaling" under the supervision of Dr. Robert Nissen.

Aren't you a tech person? Why a biochemistry degree?

Many years ago, I thought I wanted to go to medical school, so I took the MCAT and GRE, submitted applications, and got as far as attending interviews before deciding I didn't really want to do that.

So I went back to school to learn about computers! I'm currently a full time student working towards a degree in Computer Science.

Don't get me wrong though; I have an immense love for chemistry! I also believe it is a fantastic starting point for understanding anything about aything. Except for computers. They're powered by magic.

What kind of work do you do?

I mainly tutor in a number of STEM subjects. I also do some consulting and contracting work, mostly programming.

Do you plan to be proximal to academia forever?

Yeah, basically. I am fueled primarily by absurdism and existential fear, and I have an immense desire to gather as much knowledge as I can before I kick the proverbial bucket. That or upload myself to the internet. In which case, the existential fear was unwarranted.

Fueled by absurdism...?

I like to pitch hilariously terrible wordplay-based ideas to my sister. Some bear fruit, some don't. Dateline is one that's currently in the works. My latest pitch was "music for anxiety: music that gives you anxiety instead of relieving it."

You mean you don't spend your disposable income on going to school because you like learning? Lame.

Is that what you do for fun?

Pretty much everything I do is for fun. Why would I do anything unfun? Preposterous! Besides, life is fundamentally meaningless, so the things important to you have a legitimate claim to meaning as anything else.

I like reading old science books, both about science itself or the people involved. Really. I got super excited when I found a complete 26 volume Science and Invention encyclopedia from 1978. My current reads are Tuxedo Park by Jennet Conant, Discovery by Antarctic explorer Richard E. Byrd, and The Double Helix by James Watson.
